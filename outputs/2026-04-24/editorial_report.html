Section 09

Methodology

Data collection, analysis approach, and known limitations.

Data Collection. Articles were scraped from 7 Black media publications (The Grio, The Root, NewsOne, Capital B News, Blavity, Essence, Ebony) using RSS feeds as the primary method and HTML scraping as a fallback. Only articles with a publication date within the 30-day window ending April 24, 2026 were included. Each article record captures: title, snippet/description, publication, date, category, and URL.
Topic Matching. Each article's title, description, and category were matched against a curated dictionary of 12 social conflict topics. Each topic contains 20–35 seed phrases. Matching uses whole-word regular expression search (case-insensitive) with optional suffix flexibility (plurals, verb forms). Articles can match multiple topics simultaneously.
TF-IDF Keyword Extraction. Term Frequency–Inverse Document Frequency (TF-IDF) scoring was applied to the full corpus using scikit-learn's TfidfVectorizer (n-grams 1–3, min_df=2, max_df=0.85, sublinear TF normalization). Scores represent a term's relative prominence in this period compared to its overall distribution across the corpus.
Limitations. (1) Topic matching is constrained by the seed phrase dictionary — articles using different terminology for the same issue may not be captured. (2) RSS feed snippets vary in length and completeness by publication; full article body text is not analyzed. (3) The 30-day rolling window does not capture longer-term trends. (4) Coverage rates reflect the share of articles that explicitly reference tracked topics, not the total editorial attention given to those topics.
